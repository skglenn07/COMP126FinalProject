Diseases & Treatments

Rust Fungus

Rust fungus shows up as reddish-brown or orange powdery spots on leaves and stems. It likes warm, wet conditions and can affect various plants. To treat, remove infected plant parts, give your plants enough space for air, and use diluted hydrogen peroxide or fungicides made for rust. Keep an eye out for early signs and act quickly to keep your plants healthy as this fungus spreads quickly.

Mild

Yellowing Leaves

Yellowing leaves usually means something's off. It could be because the plant is missing important nutrients, the soil doesn't drain well, or you're watering too much. Plants need a green pigment called chlorophyll for photosynthesis, and when they lack nutrients the leaves turn yellow. To treat, water your plants correctly, and make sure the soil drains properly.

Mild

Powdery Mildew

Powdery mildew is a common fungus affecting houseplants, seen as a powdery white coating on leaves. It thrives in warm, dry conditions and is caused by poor air circulation, high humidity, and crowded plant arrangements. To treat it, improve air flow by spacing plants apart, maintain moderate humidity, and avoid overhead watering. Use fungicidal sprays with neem oil or potassium bicarbonate as directed. Regularly check for signs of powdery mildew to keep your indoor plants healthy.

Moderate

Root Rot

Root rot in houseplants arises from excess moisture, promoting fungal growth in the roots due to overwatering, poor drainage, and water-retaining soil. To treat root rot, let the soil dry between waterings, ensure good drainage in pots, and use well-draining soil. Trim affected roots, establish a balanced watering routine, and improve ventilation. Regularly monitor soil moisture to promptly address overwatering and maintain the health of indoor plants.
